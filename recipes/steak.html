Steak Medium Rare

Home Page

Two pieces of a brilliant medium rare steak

Savor the exquisite taste of a perfectly seared medium rare steak, a dish celebrated for its balance of tenderness, juiciness, and robust beef flavor. Cooked to perfection, this steak features a deeply caramelized crust with a warm, reddish-pink center, making it a favorite among steak lovers and fine-dining enthusiasts.

Ingredients

  • Steak – (Ribeye, Sirloin, Filet Mignon, or New York Strip) - 1-inch thick
  • Salt – (Preferably coarse sea salt or kosher salt)
  • Black Pepper – (Freshly ground for best flavor)
  • Butter – (Unsalted, for basting)
  • Garlic – (2-3 cloves, smashed or minced)
  • Fresh Herbs – (Thyme, rosemary, or parsley for aromatics)
  • Olive Oil – (Or another high smoke point oil like avocado oil)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Steak (30-40 minutes before cooking)
    • Take the steak out of the fridge and let it come to room temperature (important for even cooking).
    • Pat it dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture (helps in getting a good crust).
    • Season generously on both sides with coarse salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  2. Preheat the Pan (High Heat)
    • Place a cast iron skillet or a heavy pan on high heat for 3-5 minutes until it’s smoking hot.
    • Add 1 tablespoon of olive oil (or another high smoke point oil) and swirl it around.
  3. Sear the Steak (2-3 minutes per side)
    • Carefully place the steak in the hot pan and do not move it for at least 2 minutes (this helps build a crust).
    • Flip the steak using tongs after 2-3 minutes and sear the other side.
    • Press it down lightly to ensure even contact with the pan.
  4. Baste with Butter & Aromatics (Last 1-2 minutes)
    • Reduce heat to medium-low.
    • Add 2 tablespoons of butter, smashed garlic cloves, and fresh thyme or rosemary to the pan.
    • Tilt the pan slightly and use a spoon to continuously baste (scoop the melted butter and pour it over the steak) for about 1 minute per side.
  5. Check Temperature for Doneness
    • Insert a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the steak:
      • Rare → 120-125°F (49-52°C)
      • Medium-Rare → 130-135°F (54-57°C)
      • Medium → 140-145°F (60-63°C)
  6. Rest the Steak (5-10 minutes)
    • Remove the steak from the pan and place it on a cutting board.
    • Loosely cover with foil and let it rest for at least 5 minutes (this allows juices to redistribute).
  7. Slice & Serve
    • Slice the steak against the grain for maximum tenderness.
    • Serve with your favorite side dishes (e.g., mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a fresh salad).
